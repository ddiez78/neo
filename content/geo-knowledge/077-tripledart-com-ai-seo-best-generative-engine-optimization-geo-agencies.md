How to Choose the Best GEO Agency for Your Brand

Not every company needs the same kind of GEO partner. Here’s how to choose without getting lost in the promises.

Step 1: Get Clear on What “Success” Means for You

Ask yourself:

Do you want AI citations or qualified pipeline?
Are you optimizing for brand discovery or buyer intent?
Do you need strategy, execution, or both?

If the agency can’t connect GEO work to a business outcome, that’s a red flag.

Step 2: Look Beyond “AI SEO” Claims

Plenty of agencies say they “do AI SEO.” Fewer can explain:

How LLMs evaluate trust
How entity signals work
Why some brands get cited and others don’t

Evaluate whether the agency has dedicated GEO services or has simply rebranded its SEO offering.

Step 3: Match the Agency to Your Stage
Early-stage or startup: You need flexibility and fast learning loops
Mid-market: You need systems and repeatability
Enterprise: You need technical depth and governance

The best GEO agencies are great at saying no when they’re not the right fit.

Step 4: Demand Proof (Not Promises)

Look for:

Before-and-after visibility examples
AI-driven traffic performance
Conversion or pipeline impact

Ask to see content they've produced that currently appears as a citation in ChatGPT or Perplexity.

Why Are Traditional SEO Agencies Struggling?



While there are many conceptual overlaps between SEO and GEO, search behavior itself is now significantly different from what it used to be. Despite the rise of LLMs like ChatGPT and Gemini, most SEO agencies are still operating on a decade-old playbook. 

These agencies optimize your brand for ranking tactics rather than conversational discovery, which is crucial for GEO. This is the biggest disconnect that is costing brands visibility in AI-driven search. This is an issue plaguing even the biggest, most reputable agencies and is an evolutionary mismatch.

Legacy agencies are held back by four big problems:


Outdated focus: Prioritizing keywords and backlinks over authority, contextual clarity, structured content, or semantic alignment

Mismatched metrics: Rankings and impressions are still the gold standard, even though AI tools prioritize answer visibility and the quality of downstream engagement

Expertise Gaps: Most of their team know what works for SEO, but are not up to date with the science of GEO

Content Specialization: GEO’s backbone is content, and it requires the right mix of branding, language, and structure. LLMs work with a stronger context than Google

FAQs
Is GEO replacing SEO?

No, GEO is evolving SEO. It complements traditional SEO by optimizing content for AI engines like ChatGPT and Gemini, not replacing core search strategies.

What is the difference between SEO and GEO?

SEO targets traditional search engines like Google. GEO focuses on optimizing for AI-generated answers in tools like ChatGPT, emphasizing structure and context more strongly.

How long do GEO agency engagements typically last?

Most GEO agency engagements initially run for 3–6 months, with ongoing retainers recommended for long-term visibility and AI-driven growth.

How much should I budget for professional GEO services?

Budgets typically range from $5,000 to $15,000/month, depending on your goals, existing content maturity, and deliverables like blog posts, audits, and AI structuring.

Do I need an agency if I already have internal SEO expertise?

If your team isn’t trained in structuring content for AI tools or tracking LLM visibility, partnering with a GEO agency adds specialized firepower.

How do I measure GEO agency success?

Track AI citations, ChatGPT traffic, conversion quality, and performance across traditional and AI search platforms.
