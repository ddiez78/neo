Citations are the new backlinks. When an LLM answers a buyer question, the sources it pulls from – the URLs it footnotes, the Reddit threads it paraphrases, the listicles it ranks against – decide whether your brand is one of the trusted answers or one of the also-rans. Citation monitoring is its own discipline now: knowing not just whether you appear, but which exact pages an AI quotes when it talks about your category.

This guide covers five tools worth evaluating if the job is citation-level monitoring across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Google AI Overviews, AI Mode, Copilot, and Claude. The five tools: Peec AI, Ahrefs, Semrush, Scrunch AI, AthenaHQ. Each gets a section with what stands out about the citation product, what rivals say, what shows up on G2 where reviews exist, an honest set of pros and cons, and a verdict at the end.

## What real citation monitoring requires

An AI search citation monitoring tool that does not do these five things is a partial product. Below we present the criteria:

#### URL-level detection, not just domain-level

Domain-level citation tracking tells you “your site was cited by ChatGPT 14 times this week.” URL-level tracking tells you “ChatGPT cited your /pricing page 8 times, your blogpost 4 times, and your blog post on AI Overviews 2 times.” The difference is operational. Domain-level is a vanity metric; URL-level lets you ask which content earns citations and double down. Specialist tools (Peec AI, Scrunch AI, AthenaHQ) ship URL-level citations. Most SEO suites still report at domain level.

#### Source diversity coverage

LLMs cite editorial domains, Reddit, Wikipedia, industry glossaries, listicles, vendor pages, NYT and Forbes-class news outlets, and increasingly YouTube and LinkedIn. A citation monitoring tool that only watches editorial sources misses the half of AI-search ground-truth that lives in Reddit and listicles. Coverage matters because optimisation strategy follows source mix – if Reddit is the No.1 cited domain on Perplexity (which Semrush’s own research found), Reddit thread engagement becomes a real workflow, not a side project.

#### Citation gap analysis

Where competitors are cited and the brand is absent. This is the highest-value diagnostic in the category and most tools either lack it natively or hide it behind their highest tier. The buyer wants to know: in the prompts your category cares about, which sources are quoting your competitors instead of you, and what does each gap require to close.

#### Daily refresh

Citation source pools shift faster than most teams realise. A new industry article can start appearing in ChatGPT citations within hours of publication; a Reddit thread that pulled citations on Monday may be replaced by a fresher comparison page by Friday; competitor pricing pages get re-indexed and surface in answers the same week they ship. Weekly tracking lags those movements by 4-7 days – by the time the data lands in a Friday report, the citation landscape has already moved. A real citation tool refreshes daily on the entry plan, not as an Enterprise upcharge.

#### Per-engine citation breakdown

ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and AI Overviews draw on different source pools. ChatGPT may cite your blog post heavily while Perplexity favours a competitor’s Reddit thread on the same query. A unified citation score smooths over the divergence; per-engine breakdowns show where the source-mix battle is being won or lost.

## Features worth evaluating beyond the basics

*   **Listicle citation tracking** – which “best X” articles are cited and by which engine
*   **Reddit and UGC citation tracking** – the AI-search analogue of forum links
*   **Self-promotional listicle detection** – filtering out vendor-curated lists that bias citation counts
*   **Earned-media targeting** – turning competitor citation data into a PR pitch list
*   **Citation source authority scoring** – which domains an AI treats as trusted in your category
*   **Evidence logs** – raw LLM responses stored for audit when a citation pattern shifts

### 2. Ahrefs

Ahrefs ships two distinct products into the citation monitoring category. Brand Radar is the breadth play, included in all paid Ahrefs plans, runs against an index of 371M+ search-backed prompts, and maps citation visibility at category scale. Custom Prompts is the depth play, an add-on starting at $50/mo that lets you track exact buyer-defined prompts and the citations they generate.

#### What stands out

The structural advantage is the dataset. Brand Radar’s 371M+ prompt index is the largest breadth dataset on the market for industry-level citation benchmarking. If a brand wants to know whether its category has 10 competitors or 200 fighting for ChatGPT citations, Brand Radar surfaces the answer at scale. Custom Prompts then narrows the view to the prompts a buyer actually cares about, with overage pricing dropping from $0.020/check on Basic to $0.010/check on Scale – the cheapest per-check rate in this comparison.

The AI-generated prompt suggestions feature also stands out. The tool can read a competitor comparison page or a pricing page and propose prompts a brand should be tracked for. That automation saves an SEO lead 90 minutes a week of manual prompt research.

#### Key features

*   Brand Radar: 371M+ search-backed prompt index, included in all paid Ahrefs plans
*   Custom Prompts: 83 prompts/day on Basic to 833 prompts/day on Scale
*   Citation tracking with brand mentions, citations, impressions, AI Share of Voice
*   Fanout query discovery
*   Prompt grouping by topic, funnel stage, or campaign
*   AI-generated prompt suggestions from competitor comparisons and pricing data
*   Switch between the Ahrefs 371M+ database and custom prompts
*   API access

#### What others say

The methodology question is the one hardest to dismiss. Brand Radar’s index is built from Google’s People Also Ask questions, refreshed monthly, with Google properties (AI Overviews, AI Mode, Gemini) accounting for over 70% of the dataset. PAA questions are how people search Google, not how they prompt ChatGPT or Perplexity, and the two distributions are different in ways that matter for citation tracking. If a brand wants citation data on ChatGPT specifically, the relevant signal is what people actually type into ChatGPT, not what Google indexed as a related question.

Functional gaps surface consistently in side-by-side evaluations: no native sentiment analysis, no native competitor gap analysis, no custom-prompt tagging, exports capped at 1,000 rows.

A second structural critique: engine specificity is not detailed on the Custom Prompts pricing page. “Available platforms” is listed without explicit model coverage, which is unusual for a category where engine-by-engine matters. And the cost-of-entry is the Ahrefs paid plan ($129/mo Lite) plus Custom Prompts, not the $50 add-on advertised in isolation.

The argument worth making in Ahrefs’s favour: Brand Radar is free with paid Ahrefs plans. For an SEO team already paying Ahrefs, the breadth view comes at no incremental cost – the catch is understanding what the dataset actually represents.

#### G2 reviews

Brand Radar and Custom Prompts are not separately listed on G2. The broader Ahrefs platform has its own G2 profile, but it reflects the SEO suite (rank tracking, backlink analysis, site audit), not the AI products. Standalone reviewer voice for the citation products has not yet accumulated.

#### Best for

*   SEO teams already paying for Ahrefs that want citation breadth added at no incremental cost
*   High-volume prompt tracking at the lowest per-check rate (Scale plan, $0.010/check)
*   Buyers who want industry-wide breadth (Brand Radar) before narrowing to custom prompts

#### Pros

*   371M+ prompt index is the largest breadth dataset in this comparison
*   Brand Radar is included in paid Ahrefs plans at no incremental cost
*   Custom Prompts overage pricing scales down with volume
*   AI-generated prompt suggestions reduce manual research overhead

#### Cons

*   Underlying dataset is PAA-derived monthly, not real LLM prompt traffic daily – misaligned with citation tracking on ChatGPT and Perplexity specifically
*   Engine list on Custom Prompts pricing page is not specified
*   Real cost-of-entry is the Ahrefs paid plan ($129/mo Lite) plus Custom Prompts
*   AI visibility is a Brand Radar / Custom Prompts pair rather than a single integrated product
*   No standalone AI-visibility G2 reviews to triangulate against

#### Pricing

Ahrefs offers Custom Prompts as a three-tier add-on. Basic is $50/mo (83 prompts/day, 1 platform, 1 location, +2,500 checks/mo, overage at $0.020/check). Growth is $100/mo (233 prompts/day, multiple platforms, +7,000 checks/mo, overage at $0.015/check). Scale is $250/mo (833 prompts/day, multiple platforms, +25,000 checks/mo, overage at $0.010/check). ## Where citation monitoring is heading

Three trends are reshaping the citation layer in the next 18 months.

The first is **per-engine source-pool divergence**. ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and AI Overviews are increasingly drawing on different source pools. A unified citation score smooths over the divergence; tools that surface per-engine source breakdowns will win the buyers actually running source-diversity programmes. The single-number citation count is the OB-GYN-of-the-internet metric: technically true, operationally useless.

The second is **the listicle layer**. AI engines lean heavily on “best X” articles when answering buyer questions. Knowing which listicles are cited – and identifying self-promotional vs editorial lists – is becoming a primary citation workflow. Tools that ship listicle-aware extraction (with self-promotional filtering) will have an advantage as listicles consolidate as the dominant AI training source for category questions.

The third is **citation control through llms.txt and structured delivery**. The next wave of tools will ship the optimisation side of citation: server-side delivery (Scrunch’s AXP), llms.txt management (AthenaHQ), and AI-bot-aware site audits (Peec AI, Scrunch AI). Monitoring without control is becoming a half-product; tools that close the loop into “and here is what to fix” will dominate the buying conversation.

## FAQ

**What is the difference between domain-level and URL-level citation tracking?**

Domain-level tracking tells you which sites are cited; URL-level tracking tells you which exact pages are cited. The difference is operational. Domain-level is a vanity metric (“our site was cited 14 times this week”); URL-level lets you ask which content earns citations, double down on what works, and identify which competitor pages are pulling citations away from yours. Specialist tools (Peec AI, Scrunch AI, AthenaHQ) ship URL-level on standard plans. Most SEO suites still report at domain level only. For serious citation work, URL-level is the floor.

**How often should citation data refresh to be useful?**

Daily for active programmes; weekly only for slow-moving brands. Citation graphs rewrite themselves every few days, and source pools shift as new content gets indexed by AI engines. Peec AI offers daily refresh on every Brands plan from $95/mo Starter with no upcharge to unlock. Scrunch AI and AthenaHQ also support daily on standard plans. Ahrefs Brand Radar refreshes monthly (PAA index update cadence). Verify the exact refresh frequency on the plan you are evaluating before committing.

**Do I need both Brand Radar (Ahrefs) and a specialist for citation monitoring?**

Brand Radar is the breadth view (371M+ prompts at the category level, monthly refresh, PAA-derived). A specialist (Peec AI, Scrunch AI, AthenaHQ) gives URL-level depth on the prompts the brand actually cares about, with daily refresh on actual LLM traffic. Many serious teams run both: Brand Radar for category-level benchmarking and trend mapping, a specialist for prompt-level citation diagnostics and gap analysis. Whether the combined cost is justified depends on programme scale.

**Why is Reddit citation tracking important?**

Reddit is the No.1 cited domain on Perplexity at 4% share and No.2 on SearchGPT at 13%, per Semrush’s own primary research. AI engines treat Reddit threads as authentic-voice signal and surface them when answering category questions. Citation monitoring tools that only track editorial domains miss the half of AI-search ground-truth that lives in Reddit and similar UGC. Peec AI’s Earned Media module specifically routes Off-Page recommendations to relevant Reddit threads. Tools without UGC citation tracking deliver an incomplete picture.

**Can citation monitoring data integrate with my existing BI and reporting tools?**

Yes – depth varies sharply across the five tools. Peec AI ships a Looker Studio connector on every Brands plan from Advanced, REST API on Enterprise, and an MCP server on every plan with built-in slash commands for live querying inside Claude, Cursor, VS Code, or n8n. Scrunch AI offers Looker Studio on every plan and API access. AthenaHQ adds BI dashboard integrations (Tableau, Power BI, Looker) on Enterprise. Semrush AI Visibility currently lacks native CSV or Looker Studio export. Ahrefs Custom Prompts has API access. The integration depth often determines whether citation data ends up driving decisions or sitting in a dashboard nobody opens.
